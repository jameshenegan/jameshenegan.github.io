Websites as Educational Resources

  1. Penn State Statistics. Notes available for various statistics courses. Graduate courses include Applied Statistics (500), Regression Methods (501), ANOVA and Design of Experiments (502), Design of Experiments (503), Analysis of Discrete Data (504), Applied Multivariate Statistical Analysis (505), Sampling Theory and Methods (506), Epidemiological Research Methods (507), Applied Data Mining & Statistical Learning (508), Design and Analysis of Clinical Trials (509), Applied Time Series Analysis (510), Statistical Analysis of Genomics Data (555), Introduction to Applied Statistics (800). They also have some undergraduate courses which focus on SAS (480-483) and R (484-485).
  2. UCLA Institute for Digial Research and Education (IDRE). Data Analysis Examples and Annotated Output.
  3. Patrick Breheny: The Iowa Courses include Survival Data Analysis, High Dimensional Data Analysis, Introduction to Biostatistics, and Biostatistical Methods. The Kentucky Courses include Advanced Regression, Bayesian Modeling in Biostatistics, Nonparametric Statistics, Biostatistics, Applied Statistical Modeling for Medicine and Publich Health.
  4. Khan Academy: Statistics and Probability
