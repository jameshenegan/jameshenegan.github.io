Resources

These are some of the most helpful books, websites, tools and online courses that I have encountered since August 2018.

Books

  1. Introduction to Categorical Data Analysis by Alan Agresti. This nontechnical book is a pleasure to read. It covers topics such as Logistic Regression, Multicategory Logit Models, and Loglinear Models.
  2. Statistics and Data Analysis: From Elementary to Intermediate by Ajit Tamhane and Dorothy Dunlop.
    3. This book strikes a good balance between theory and application. It covers a wide variety of topics, including the basic concepts of inference, simple and multiple linear regression, analysis of experiments, and likelihood methods.
  3. An Introduction to Statistical Learning by Gareth James, Daniela Witten, Trevor Hastie, and Robert Tibshirani.
    4. Topics covered include classification, resampling methods, tree-based methods, support vector machines, and clustering.
  4. R for Data Science by Garrett Grolemund and Hadley Wickham. This book shows how one can use the Tidyverse to accomplish various tasks associated with Data Science.
  5. Python Data Science Handbook by Jake VanderPlas. This book shows how one can use Python to accomplish various tasks associated with Data Science.
  6. Statistical Rethinking by Richard McElreath. An excellent introduction to Bayesian Data Analysis.
  7. The Lady Tasting Tea: How Statistics Revolutionized Science in the Twentieth Century by David Salsburg. This nontechnical book gives interesting biographical information about many of the people who helped develop the field of Statistics.

Websites

  1. Penn State Statistics. Notes available for various statistics courses. Graduate courses include Applied Statistics (500), Regression Methods (501), ANOVA and Design of Experiments (502), Design of Experiments (503), Analysis of Discrete Data (504), Applied Multivariate Statistical Analysis (505), Sampling Theory and Methods (506), Epidemiological Research Methods (507), Applied Data Mining & Statistical Learning (508), Design and Analysis of Clinical Trials (509), Applied Time Series Analysis (510), Statistical Analysis of Genomics Data (555), Introduction to Applied Statistics (800). They also have some undergraduate courses which focus on SAS (480-483) and R (484-485).
  2. UCLA Institute for Digial Research and Education (IDRE). Data Analysis Examples and Annotated Output.
  3. Patrick Breheny: The Iowa Courses include Survival Data Analysis, High Dimensional Data Analysis, Introduction to Biostatistics, and Biostatistical Methods. The Kentucky Courses include Advanced Regression, Bayesian Modeling in Biostatistics, Nonparametric Statistics, Biostatistics, Applied Statistical Modeling for Medicine and Publich Health.

Online Courses

  1. Probabilistic Graphical Models Specialization. A Coursera course offered by Stanford, taught by Daphne Koller.
  2. A Crash Course in Causality: Inferring Causal Effects from Observational Data. This Coursea course is taught by Jason Roy, the Chair of the Department of Biostatistics and Epidemilogy at the Rutgers School of Public Health.
  3. Build Data Visualizations with D3.js & Firebase. This Udemy Course was taught by Shaun Pelling (also known as the Net Ninja).
  4. Introduction to Computational Thinking and Data Science. This course is given by MIT Open Courseware. It provides an introduction to Optimization, Graph-theoretic models, Stochastic Thinking, Monte Carlo Simulation, and Machine Learning.
  5. CS50's Web Programming with Python and JavaScript. Given by Harvard University, this course discusses the design and implementatino of web applications with Python, JavaScript, and SQL.
