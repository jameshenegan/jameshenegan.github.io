IT Incident Management Process Documentation

Document Version: 1.0
Last Updated: February 1, 2025
Process Owner: IT Service Manager
Contributors: IT Support Team, Network Operations, Security Team

1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to provide a structured framework for identifying, reporting, and resolving IT incidents. The goal is to minimize downtime, restore services as quickly as possible, and ensure consistent communication throughout the process.

2. Scope

This process applies to all IT-related incidents affecting internal systems, network services, hardware, and software applications. It covers incidents ranging from minor technical glitches to major system outages.

3. Definitions

  • Incident: An unplanned interruption or reduction in quality of an IT service.
  • Incident Management: The process of restoring normal service operation as quickly as possible while minimizing the impact on business operations.
  • Severity Levels: Categories assigned based on the impact and urgency of an incident (e.g., Critical, High, Medium, Low).
  • Escalation: The process of involving higher-level support or management when an incident cannot be resolved promptly.

5. Detailed Process Steps

5.1. Incident Identification & Reporting

  • Step 1: Incident Detection

    • Responsible: All Employees / Monitoring Systems
    • Action: Report any unusual behavior or system downtime immediately via the IT service portal or hotline.
    • Timeline: As soon as the incident is detected.

  • Step 2: Incident Logging

    • Responsible: IT Support Desk
    • Action: Log the incident in the incident management system, including details such as time, affected systems, and reporter information.
    • Timeline: Within 15 minutes of detection.

  • Step 3: Incident Categorization and Prioritization

    • Responsible: IT Support Desk
    • Action: Categorize the incident (e.g., network, hardware, software) and assign a severity level based on impact and urgency.
    • Timeline: Immediately after logging the incident.

5.2. Incident Diagnosis & Investigation

  • Step 1: Initial Diagnosis

    • Responsible: IT Support Technician
    • Action: Conduct an initial assessment to determine the scope and potential cause of the incident.
    • Timeline: Within 30 minutes of logging.

  • Step 2: Escalation (if needed)

    • Responsible: IT Support Technician / IT Service Manager
    • Action: If the incident exceeds the technician’s scope, escalate to a specialized team (e.g., network operations or security).
    • Timeline: Within 1 hour of initial diagnosis.

  • Step 3: Root Cause Analysis

    • Responsible: Escalated Team
    • Action: Perform a detailed investigation to identify the underlying cause of the incident.
    • Timeline: Depending on incident severity (Critical incidents should have preliminary analysis within 2 hours).

5.3. Incident Resolution & Recovery

  • Step 1: Resolution Implementation

    • Responsible: IT Support or Specialized Team
    • Action: Apply the necessary fixes or workarounds to resolve the incident.
    • Timeline: As soon as the root cause is identified.

  • Step 2: Service Recovery

    • Responsible: IT Support Desk
    • Action: Verify that the service has been restored to normal operating conditions.
    • Timeline: Immediately following resolution implementation.

  • Step 3: Communication with Stakeholders

    • Responsible: IT Service Manager
    • Action: Inform relevant stakeholders (affected departments, management) about the incident resolution and any follow-up actions required.
    • Timeline: Within 30 minutes of service recovery.

5.4. Incident Closure & Review

  • Step 1: Incident Closure

    • Responsible: IT Support Desk
    • Action: Close the incident in the management system once verification is complete, ensuring all details are updated.
    • Timeline: Within 1 hour of service recovery.

  • Step 2: Post-Incident Review

    • Responsible: IT Service Manager
    • Action: Conduct a review meeting with the involved teams to discuss what happened, how it was handled, and what improvements can be made.
    • Timeline: Within 5 business days of incident closure.

  • Step 3: Documentation & Process Improvement

    • Responsible: IT Service Manager
    • Action: Update the incident documentation with lessons learned and adjust processes as needed for future improvements.
    • Timeline: Following the post-incident review.

6. Roles and Responsibilities

Role Responsibility
All Employees Detect and report potential incidents immediately.
IT Support Desk Log, categorize, and initiate first-level diagnosis of reported incidents.
IT Support Technician Perform initial diagnosis and escalate incidents as required.
Specialized Teams Conduct in-depth root cause analysis and implement resolution actions.
IT Service Manager Oversee the entire incident management process, facilitate communication, and lead post-incident reviews.

7. Process Metrics and KPIs

  • Incident Response Time: Time from incident detection to logging.
  • Resolution Time: Time from incident logging to service recovery.
  • Escalation Rate: Percentage of incidents that require escalation to a specialized team.
  • Post-Incident Review Completion: Percentage of incidents followed by a review meeting within 5 business days.
  • User Satisfaction: Feedback from affected users post-resolution.

8. Notes and Considerations

  • Communication:
    Regular updates should be provided to affected users throughout the incident lifecycle to maintain transparency.
  • Training:
    Ensure IT support staff and specialized teams receive regular training on the incident management process.
  • Continuous Improvement:
    Use feedback and post-incident reviews to refine and improve the process over time.
  • Compliance:
    Verify that incident handling adheres to any relevant industry regulations and internal policies.
