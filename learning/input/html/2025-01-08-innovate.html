2. Revised Draft

Overview
Leading tech companies like Microsoft and Google are introducing a new type of product known as Generative Search Engines (or “Generative Engines”). These tools combine traditional web search with the summarization power of Large Language Models (LLMs). When users submit a question to a Generative Engine, the answer is created by summarizing and citing content discovered through conventional search.

Why This Matters
Being cited by a Generative Engine boosts a company’s visibility, brand awareness, and credibility. In a competitive market, higher visibility translates into stronger marketing reach and more potential customers. As these engines become more popular, it is only natural that companies will want to ensure they are featured prominently in AI-generated summaries.

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)
We use the term Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) to describe methods that help a company’s website appear in answers generated by LLMs. It differs from traditional Search Engine Optimization (SEO). While SEO focuses on improving rankings in search results, GEO targets how well a Generative Engine summarizes and cites a website’s content.

Our Project
We anticipate that Generative Engines will rapidly gain traction. To prepare, we built a custom Generative Engine to evaluate our company’s website content. Our findings revealed opportunities for improvement. For instance, when we asked “What products are offered by [our company]?”, our website ranked fourth out of several sources. This suggests that three other sites provided clearer or more authoritative information on our products.

Next, we experimented with changes to our website content to see if we could raise our standing in the Generative Engine’s summarized results. By making targeted adjustments, we increased our site’s visibility by 154% for that specific question.

What We Offer

  1. A Measurement Framework
    We have developed a method to gauge how effectively our website answers life insurance questions compared to competing sites.

  2. A Testing Methodology
    Using the same framework, we can measure the impact of content updates and ensure they improve our site’s standing within Generative Engines.

Key Benefits

  • Low-Cost, High ROI: Our approach relies on publicly accessible information and straightforward testing strategies, resulting in minimal additional expense.
  • Safe Use of LLMs: We only submit data that is already publicly available, so there are no privacy or security risks.
  • Future-Proof Strategy: As Generative Engines become more widespread, our company is positioned to “get in on the ground floor” and stay ahead of the competition.

Conclusion
We believe that investing in Generative Engine Optimization now will yield significant benefits as LLM-based search grows in popularity. Our framework and testing methodology can help our company strengthen its online presence, boost brand credibility, and capture more public attention—all while maintaining a cost-effective, low-risk approach to leveraging AI technologies.
