Companies like Google and Microsoft are now offering “Generative Engines” that combine traditional web search with Large Language Models (LLMs) to produce AI-generated summaries. These summaries appear in a prominent position on search engine results pages, pushing regular search listings further down. Consequently, being featured in these AI summaries has become critical for boosting visibility, brand awareness, and credibility.

To help our organization adapt, we introduce Generative Engine Optimization (GEO), which focuses on making website content more likely to be cited and summarized by LLMs—a key difference from conventional Search Engine Optimization (SEO). As part of our work, we developed a custom Generative Engine to assess our own site’s performance. Initially, our website ranked fourth when asked, “What products does [our company] offer?”—indicating that other websites provided more compelling explanations of our offerings. After refining an offline version of our content, we improved our visibility by 154% for that same query.

Our project makes three key contributions:

  1. A measurement framework that benchmarks how effectively our content answers industry-relevant questions.
  2. A testing methodology that quantifies the impact of targeted website updates on Generative Engine results.
  3. An LLM-based content generation approach, which uses AI to create and refine website content for better GEO performance.

Because all data used in this process is publicly approved, there are minimal privacy or security concerns. We believe GEO offers a low-cost, high-impact way to stay competitive in an increasingly AI-driven search ecosystem.

Background

Leading tech companies like Microsoft and Google are introducing a new type of product known as Generative Search Engines (or “Generative Engines”). These tools combine traditional web search with the summarization power of Large Language Models (LLMs). When users submit a query to a search engine such as Google or Bing—which now integrate Generative Engines—the answer is produced by summarizing and citing content discovered through conventional search.

Why This Matters

Google and Bing showcase their AI-generated summaries at the top of the search engine results page, with traditional results appearing below. This prime placement increases visibility for companies referenced in those summaries, enhancing brand awareness and credibility. In a competitive market, higher visibility translates into stronger marketing reach and more potential customers. As these engines gain popularity, it is only natural that companies will want to ensure they appear prominently in AI-driven results.

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)

We use the term Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) to describe strategies that help a company’s website appear in answers generated by LLMs. Unlike traditional Search Engine Optimization (SEO)—which focuses on climbing the list of organic search results—GEO centers on how effectively a Generative Engine cites and summarizes a website’s content. Conventional SEO techniques do little to improve AI-generated summaries.

Our Project

To prepare for the rapid rise of Generative Engines, we developed a custom Generative Engine to evaluate our company’s website content. Our findings revealed opportunities for improvement. For example, when we asked, “What products are offered by [our company]?”, our site ranked fourth among several sources—indicating that three other websites presented clearer or more authoritative information on our own products.

Next, we made targeted changes to an offline version of our website content to see if we could improve our visibility within the Generative Engine’s summarized results. By fine-tuning the information presented, we boosted our site’s visibility by 154% for that specific question.

What We Offer

  1. Measurement Framework
    We have created a method to gauge how effectively our website answers life insurance questions relative to competing sites.

  2. Testing Methodology
    This approach also enables us to measure the impact of any content updates, ensuring improvements in our standing with Generative Engines.

  3. Future Enhancements
    Our experiments open the door to enhancing future versions of our existing content management system by leveraging LLMs. These models can create optimized content or suggest GEO-friendly modifications to existing text.

Key Benefits

  • Low-Cost, High ROI: Our approach relies on publicly accessible information and straightforward testing strategies, keeping expenses minimal.
  • Safe Use of LLMs: We only share data that is already public or approved for public consumption, eliminating privacy and security risks.
  • Future-Proof Strategy: As Generative Engines gain traction, our company is positioned to “get in on the ground floor” and maintain a competitive edge.

Conclusion

By investing in Generative Engine Optimization now, our company can benefit as LLM-based search grows in popularity. Our framework and testing methodology empower us to strengthen our online presence, elevate brand credibility, and capture greater public attention—all with a cost-effective, low-risk approach to AI technology.
