Colorado Division of Insurance – Regulation 10-1-1 (effective Nov 14 2023) Governance and Risk-Management Framework Requirements for Life Insurers’ Use of External Consumer Data & Information Sources (ECDIS), Algorithms, and Predictive Models

What the rule does

Section Key points
Authority & Scope Issued under §§ 10-1-109 and 10-3-1104.9 C.R.S.; applies to every life insurer licensed in Colorado. (sos.state.co.us)
Purpose Ensure that any ECDIS-driven algorithm or model is “reasonably designed to prevent unfair discrimination,” especially with respect to race. (sos.state.co.us)
Definitions Clarifies “Algorithm,” “External Consumer Data and Information Source,” “Predictive Model,” “Unfair Discrimination,” etc.—capturing items such as credit scores, social-media data, IoT data, and risk scores derived from them. (sos.state.co.us)
Governance & Risk-Management Framework (Section 5) Insurers that use ECDIS/algorithms/models must build a documented, board-level framework with 13 required elements, including: 1) governing principles; 2) board or board-committee oversight; 3) senior-management accountability; 4) a cross-functional governance group; 5) policies for design, testing, deployment, and monitoring; 6) consumer-complaint protocols; 7) a risk-ranking rubric; 8-11) detailed inventories, change logs, bias-testing results, and ongoing model-drift monitoring; 12) vendor-selection controls; 13) annual framework reviews. (sos.state.co.us)
Vendor responsibility Using third-party data or models does not shift liability; the insurer must still prove compliance and supply documentation on request. (sos.state.co.us)
Reporting (Section 6) Existing users must file: • Progress report by Jun 1 2024 • Full compliance narrative (≤10 pp, officer-signed) by Dec 1 2024 and annually. Non-users file an annual attestation that no ECDIS/algorithms/models are in use. New adopters must submit the full narrative before first use. (sos.state.co.us)
Confidentiality All submitted materials are protected under § 10-3-1104.9(3)(d) C.R.S. (sos.state.co.us)
Enforcement Non-compliance may trigger civil penalties, cease-and-desist orders, or license actions. (sos.state.co.us)
Effective date Regulation in force as of November 14 2023; first documentation due December 1 2024. (sos.state.co.us)

Bottom line for life insurers

  • Build or upgrade a formal AI/data-governance program—board-owned, cross-functional, fully documented.
  • Identify, inventory, and continuously test every external data source, algorithm, or predictive model for racial bias and other unfair discrimination.
  • Document everything and be ready to hand it to the Division; the first major filings start mid-2024.
  • Stay accountable even when outsourcing: contracts with data/model vendors must enable you to meet these obligations.

Failing to do so risks fines or loss of license.
