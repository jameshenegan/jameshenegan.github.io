Script (Approx. 1–2 Minutes):

Good morning, everyone. I’m a member of the Data Analytics team here at [OUR COMPANY], and I’d like to quickly explain who we are and what we do. Our team is part of Corporate Data and Analytics—often called CD&A. As the name suggests, we take a broad view of the company’s data landscape rather than focusing on a single area like Underwriting, Marketing, or Claims. We want to examine and utilize the wealth of data—the goldmine—our company has collected over the years.

In essence, our job is to transform raw data into actionable insights that guide strategy and uncover new opportunities—opportunities that would otherwise remain hidden in spreadsheets or databases. We work closely with the other CD&A teams:

  • Data Engineering, which ensures our data is properly gathered and organized,
  • Business Intelligence, which creates dashboards and reports to visualize key metrics, and
  • Data Governance, which makes sure we handle everything ethically and responsibly.

However, our collaboration doesn’t stop there. We aim to partner with every business area across the company to ensure that data-driven decisions are informed by a holistic understanding of our organization.

Now, what does a data scientist on our team actually do? They combine math, programming, and deep business knowledge to analyze complex datasets, build predictive models, and spot trends before anyone else sees them. These could be advanced machine learning solutions that forecast customer behavior or statistical analyses that highlight ways to streamline processes. Data scientists then turn these findings into practical recommendations, working closely with business partners so that every insight addresses real-world needs and drives tangible benefits for the company.

Ultimately, our team’s mission is to help everyone make smarter decisions, faster. By leveraging our company’s vast data resources—and doing so responsibly under our new policy—we aim to foster innovation, improve efficiency, and deliver value across all departments. Thank you.
