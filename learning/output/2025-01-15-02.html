Let’s walk through how we tested each version of the site.

  1. Pick a question.

We selected a specific question from our list of questions related to life insurance.

  1. Choose a site version.

We decided which version of our site, either original or alternate, to include in our Generative Engine’s reference material.

  1. Run the query.

We fed the question and selected site version into our Custom Generative Engine. It gathered external references, then generated an answer that cited each source.

  1. Compute the visibility score. This gave us an objective measure we could use that told us whether the AI found our content valuable enough to be included prominently in the result.

  2. Repeat and average.

Since AI models can produce varying results, we ran multiple trials for each question-site version pair and took an average score.

This reduced the effect of random fluctuations.

Our main question was: does the new, AI-friendly version of the site out-perform the original one?

The short answer is: yes.

In nearly every question we tested, the alternate version achieved both a higher visibility score and a better ranking.

For example, when we asked, “What products are offered by our company?”, the original site scored 0.27, ranking 4th.

The alternate site ranked first and scored 0.44.

This translates to a 63% increase in visibility.

You’ll see similar improvements across other questions in your handout.

But I would like to take this opportunity to emphasize that our project as a whole isn’t really about these specific numbers.

What we’re really offering is a repeatable process for measuring and improving our presence in AI-driven searches.
