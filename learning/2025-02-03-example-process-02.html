Below is an example of process documentation for a Monthly Machine Learning Model Retraining and Inferencing Process used by a data science team. This documentation outlines the steps involved from data ingestion to model retraining, inferencing, and integration with downstream business decisions.

Monthly Machine Learning Model Retraining and Inferencing Process

Document Version: 1.0
Last Updated: February 1, 2025
Process Owner: Data Science Team Lead
Contributors: Data Engineers, Data Scientists, IT Operations, Business Stakeholders

1. Purpose

This document describes the process for monthly retraining of a classification machine learning (ML) model and executing batch inferencing when new data is received. The aim is to ensure that the model remains up-to-date with the latest data, provides accurate predictions, and supports informed business decisions.

2. Scope

This process applies to all data related to the classification model. It covers:

  • Monthly data ingestion and merging (appending new data to historical records)
  • Retraining of the classification model
  • Batch inferencing on the updated dataset
  • Communication of predictions to downstream business processes

3. Definitions

  • Data Ingestion: The process of collecting and importing new data into the existing dataset.
  • Retraining: Updating the ML model using the combined dataset (historical + new data).
  • Batch Inferencing: Running the updated model on the dataset to generate predictions.
  • Classification Model: A machine learning model that predicts categorical outcomes.
  • Downstream Business Decision: Any business process or decision that uses the model’s predictions as input.

5. Detailed Process Steps

5.1. Data Ingestion & Preparation

  • Step 1: Receive New Data

    • Responsible: Data Engineering Team
    • Action: Retrieve new data files from designated sources (e.g., API endpoint, database extract, or file drop) on a monthly basis.
    • Timeline: Upon availability of new data (approximately once a month).

  • Step 2: Validate and Clean Data

    • Responsible: Data Engineering Team
    • Action: Validate the new data for completeness, consistency, and quality. Perform necessary cleaning steps (e.g., removing duplicates, handling missing values).
    • Timeline: Within 1–2 days after new data receipt.

  • Step 3: Append New Data to Historical Dataset

    • Responsible: Data Engineering Team
    • Action: Append validated new data to the historical dataset stored in the data warehouse. Ensure data versioning and backup procedures are followed.
    • Timeline: Immediately after validation.

5.2. Model Retraining

  • Step 1: Data Splitting (Training/Validation)

    • Responsible: Data Scientists
    • Action: Split the combined dataset into training and validation sets using a pre-defined strategy (e.g., 80/20 split).
    • Timeline: Before model retraining begins.

  • Step 2: Model Retraining

    • Responsible: Data Scientists
    • Action: Retrain the classification model using the training set. Implement any necessary feature engineering or parameter tuning during retraining.
    • Timeline: Within 1–2 days after data ingestion.

  • Step 3: Model Evaluation and Validation

    • Responsible: Data Scientists
    • Action: Evaluate the retrained model using the validation set. Metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, F1 score, or AUC should be compared with previous model performance.
    • Timeline: Immediately after retraining.
    • Outcome: Approve the model for deployment if performance meets or exceeds the baseline threshold; otherwise, iterate on model adjustments.

5.3. Batch Inferencing

  • Step 1: Run Predictions on the Updated Dataset
    • Responsible: Data Science/Engineering Teams
    • Action: Deploy the retrained model in batch mode to run inferencing on the new appended dataset.
    • Timeline: Immediately after model validation.
  • Step 2: Store and Format Prediction Results
    • Responsible: Data Engineering Team
    • Action: Store the prediction results in a dedicated database or data warehouse table. Format the output to align with the requirements of downstream systems.
    • Timeline: Within a few hours post inferencing.

5.4. Integration with Business Decisions

  • Step 1: Deliver Predictions to Business Systems

    • Responsible: Data Engineering Team
    • Action: Export prediction results to the systems used by business stakeholders (e.g., dashboards, reporting tools, or decision-support applications).
    • Timeline: Immediately after storing predictions.

  • Step 2: Notify Stakeholders

    • Responsible: Data Science Team Lead
    • Action: Communicate the completion of the inferencing process and share key performance metrics and results with business stakeholders.
    • Timeline: On the same day as the inferencing process.

  • Step 3: Monitor Outcomes and Feedback

    • Responsible: Data Science Team
    • Action: Collect feedback from the business teams on the utility and impact of the predictions. Monitor business outcomes to assess the real-world performance of the model.
    • Timeline: Continuously post deployment; schedule review meetings monthly or quarterly.

6. Roles and Responsibilities

Role Responsibility
Data Engineering Team Manage data ingestion, validation, cleaning, and storage.
Data Scientists Retrain the ML model, perform model evaluation, and generate predictions.
Data Science Team Lead Oversee the retraining process, ensure quality, and coordinate with business units.
IT Operations Provide and maintain the infrastructure required for data processing and model deployment.
Business Stakeholders Use the model predictions in decision-making and provide feedback for process improvement.

7. Process Metrics and KPIs

  • Data Ingestion Timeliness: Time taken from new data availability to complete ingestion and validation.
  • Model Retraining Duration: Time from initiation of retraining to model validation.
  • Model Performance Metrics: Changes in accuracy, precision, recall, etc., compared to previous versions.
  • Inference Completion Time: Time from model deployment to the availability of prediction results.
  • Business Impact: Feedback and measurable outcomes from decisions based on the model’s predictions (e.g., improved conversion rates, cost savings).

8. Notes and Considerations

  • Version Control:
    Maintain versioning of datasets and models to enable rollback if needed.
  • Automation:
    Wherever possible, automate steps such as data validation, model retraining, and inferencing to reduce manual intervention and errors.
  • Compliance:
    Ensure data handling complies with relevant regulations (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA) and company policies.
  • Feedback Loop:
    Schedule regular review sessions with business stakeholders to continuously improve the process and model performance.
