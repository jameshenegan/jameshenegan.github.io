Employee Onboarding Process Documentation

Document Version: 1.0
Last Updated: February 1, 2025
Process Owner: Human Resources Manager
Contributors: HR Team, IT Department, Department Managers

1. Purpose

The purpose of this document is to outline the steps and responsibilities involved in onboarding new employees. This process is designed to ensure a smooth transition into the company, promote employee engagement, and provide all necessary tools and information for success.

2. Scope

This process applies to all new hires across departments. It covers pre-boarding activities, the first day, and the first 90 days of employment.

3. Definitions

  • Onboarding: The process of integrating a new employee into the organization.
  • Pre-boarding: Activities completed before the employee’s first day (e.g., documentation, IT setup).
  • Buddy Program: A system where an experienced employee is assigned to help the new hire acclimate.

5. Detailed Process Steps

5.1. Pre-boarding

  • Step 1: Send Offer Letter & Acceptance Confirmation

    • Responsible: HR Representative
    • Action: Email the offer letter and necessary documents to the candidate.
    • Timeline: Within 2 business days of candidate selection.

  • Step 2: Complete New Hire Paperwork

    • Responsible: HR Representative
    • Action: Guide the new hire to complete all required forms (tax forms, confidentiality agreements, etc.) via the onboarding portal.
    • Timeline: Prior to the start date.

  • Step 3: Set Up IT Accounts and Workspace

    • Responsible: IT Department
    • Action: Create email accounts, access credentials, and set up the workstation.
    • Timeline: At least 3 days before the start date.

  • Step 4: Schedule Orientation and Training Sessions

    • Responsible: HR Coordinator
    • Action: Schedule the orientation meeting and initial training sessions with relevant departments.
    • Timeline: Complete scheduling one week prior to the start date.

5.2. First Day

  • Step 1: Welcome Meeting with HR

    • Responsible: HR Manager
    • Action: Provide an overview of company culture, policies, and benefits.
    • Timeline: Morning session.

  • Step 2: Office Tour and Introductions

    • Responsible: Assigned Buddy
    • Action: Introduce the new hire to key areas and team members.
    • Timeline: Immediately following the welcome meeting.

  • Step 3: IT Orientation and System Access Verification

    • Responsible: IT Support
    • Action: Verify that all accounts and systems are accessible and provide a brief tutorial.
    • Timeline: Mid-morning.

  • Step 4: Introduction to Assigned Buddy

    • Responsible: HR Manager
    • Action: Formally introduce the new hire to their buddy who will assist during the initial onboarding period.
    • Timeline: By the end of the first day.

5.3. First Week

  • Step 1: Departmental Orientation

    • Responsible: Department Manager
    • Action: Provide an overview of departmental functions, roles, and workflows.
    • Timeline: During the first week.

  • Step 2: Review of Job Responsibilities and Expectations

    • Responsible: Direct Manager
    • Action: Conduct a one-on-one meeting to discuss specific responsibilities, performance expectations, and short-term goals.
    • Timeline: Within the first 3 days.

  • Step 3: Initial Training Sessions

    • Responsible: Training Coordinator
    • Action: Enroll the new hire in relevant training programs or sessions to build necessary skills.
    • Timeline: Throughout the first week.

  • Step 4: Set Up Regular Check-In Meetings

    • Responsible: Direct Manager
    • Action: Schedule weekly meetings for the first month to monitor progress and answer any questions.
    • Timeline: Beginning at the end of the first week.

5.4. First 90 Days

  • Step 1: Ongoing Training and Development Sessions

    • Responsible: Training Department
    • Action: Organize additional sessions based on the employee’s role and feedback.
    • Timeline: Monthly sessions.

  • Step 2: Regular Performance Check-Ins

    • Responsible: Direct Manager
    • Action: Hold monthly check-ins to evaluate progress, address concerns, and adjust goals as needed.
    • Timeline: Monthly.

  • Step 3: Feedback Collection from New Hire

    • Responsible: HR Manager
    • Action: Conduct surveys or one-on-one sessions to gather feedback about the onboarding experience.
    • Timeline: At the end of the first month and at the end of 90 days.

  • Step 4: End of Probation Review

    • Responsible: Direct Manager and HR Manager
    • Action: Evaluate the new hire’s performance, determine if the probationary period should be extended, or confirm permanent status.
    • Timeline: At the end of the 90-day period.

6. Roles and Responsibilities

Role Responsibility
HR Manager Overall process oversight, welcome meetings, and feedback.
HR Representative Sending offer letters, collecting paperwork, and initial setup.
IT Department Creating accounts, setting up workstations, and providing IT support.
Direct Manager Clarifying job responsibilities, setting check-in meetings, and performance reviews.
Assigned Buddy Assisting with day-to-day orientation, introductions, and ongoing support.
Training Coordinator Organizing training sessions and ensuring the new hire has access to necessary resources.

7. Process Metrics and KPIs

  • Time to Complete Onboarding: Measure the number of days from start date to completion of all onboarding tasks.
  • New Hire Satisfaction: Gather feedback through surveys at 30, 60, and 90 days.
  • Retention Rate: Track employee retention over the first 90 days.
  • Training Completion Rate: Percentage of required training sessions completed within the first month.

8. Notes and Considerations

  • Flexibility:
    Adjust the process as needed for remote employees or different departmental requirements.
  • Continuous Improvement:
    Regularly review the process metrics and feedback to identify areas for improvement.
  • Compliance:
    Ensure that all onboarding steps comply with legal and regulatory requirements.
