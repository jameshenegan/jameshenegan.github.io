About the Title. In order to perform certain statistical tasks, we will sometimes make assumptions about how a Data Generating Process (DGP) behaves. Often times, the resulting statistical analysis is based on a question that can phrased as follows:

If our assumptions about the DGP are correct,
then how likely is it that the DGP would generate the results that we have observed?

To address this question, we contrast what is expected to be produced by the DGP with what is observed through empirical evidence. In this way, we can gain an idea about the validity of our assumptions.
