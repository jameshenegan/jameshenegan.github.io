Hello, and welcome to my personal website. It is a work in progress, but the goal is to have it up and running by the end of January, 2019.

I am a doctoral candidate in the Department of Data Science at the University of Mississippi Medical Center (UMMC). I will complete the second year of my graduate studies in Data Science in May, 2020.

I obtained a PhD in Mathematics from the University of Mississippi in the Spring of 2017. My dissertation, entitled Asymptotic Properties of Polynomials Orthogonal over Multiply Connected Domains , was a work of "pure mathematics." By this, I mean that it would be difficult to apply the results of my dissertation to any problem that might arise in the real world. Soon after graduation, I became interested in learning more about tools and techniques that could be applied to real world problems. Consequently, I decided to join the Department of Data Science at UMMC in the Fall of 2018.

The courses I have taken over the past year and a half have focused on Biostatistics, Statistical Learning, and Programming. I have gained experience working with SAS, Stata, R, and Python. Currently, I prefer to use R for data cleaning and plotting, Stata for statistical analysis, and Python for Machine Learning. However, I am always willing to learn more about programming, especially if I need to do so in order to fit into a work environment.
