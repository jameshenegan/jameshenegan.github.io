Introduction

Hello, and welcome to my personal website! It is a work in progress, but the goal is to have it up and running by the end of January, 2020.

I am currently a graduate student in the Department of Data Science at the University of Mississippi Medical Center (UMMC). I will complete the second year of my graduate studies in Data Science in May, 2020.

I obtained a PhD in Mathematics from the University of Mississippi in the Spring of 2017. My dissertation, entitled Asymptotic Properties of Polynomials Orthogonal over Multiply Connected Domains , was a work of "pure mathematics." By this, I mean that it would be difficult to apply the main results of my thesis to any problem that might arise in the real world. Soon after graduation, I became interested in learning more about tools and techniques that could be applied to real world problems. Consequently, I decided to join the Department of Data Science at UMMC in the Fall of 2018.

The courses I have taken over the past year and a half have focused on Biostatistics, Statistical Learning, and Programming. I have gained experience working with SAS, Stata, R, and Python. Currently, I prefer to use R for data cleaning and plotting, Stata for statistical analysis, and Python for Machine Learning.

About the Image on the Right. Coming from a "pure math" background, I often think about the interplay between the theoretical and the empirical when working with Statistics. If they were alive today, I'd like to think that Plato would enjoy seeing how often the math in Statistics works out nicely, while Aristotle would be hounding us to make sure that the theory that we are developing actually applies to the real world.

School of Athens (detail), by Raphael. Plato is on the left, Aristotle is on the right. With his concept of form, Plato is associated with Theory. Meanwhile, Aristotle is associated with empirical evidence.
